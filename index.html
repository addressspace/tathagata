Photoacoustic Imaging with Deep Learning

Tathagata Das, Mayanglambam Suheshkumar Singh

BnBEng.LAB, School of Physics, Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India

Photoacoustic imaging (PAI) is a hybrid imaging modality that combines the high contrast of optical imaging with the deep penetration capabilities of ultrasound. In this project, we developed a novel deep learning architecture called Fast Adaptive U-Net (FAU-Net) specifically designed for photoacoustic image reconstruction and enhancement.

The FAU-Net architecture incorporates adaptive features that allow it to adjust its parameters based on the input data characteristics, resulting in superior reconstruction quality compared to traditional methods and standard U-Net architectures.

Status: Manuscript in preparation

Keywords: Photoacoustic Imaging, Deep Learning, Image Reconstruction, U-Net, Medical Imaging

Our custom-built photoacoustic imaging system consisting of a pulsed laser, ultrasound transducer array, and data acquisition system.

Figure 2: FAU-Net Architecture

The proposed Fast Adaptive U-Net architecture with adaptive feature selection modules and skip connections.

Figure 3: Reconstruction Results

Comparison of image reconstruction results using (a) Delay-and-Sum beamforming, (b) Standard U-Net, and (c) our proposed FAU-Net.

Light Sheet Microscopy Focus Stabilization

Tathagata Das, Mayanglambam Suheshkumar Singh

BnBEng.LAB, School of Physics, Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India

Light sheet fluorescence microscopy (LSFM) is a powerful imaging technique for 3D biological samples, offering reduced phototoxicity and fast acquisition speeds. However, maintaining optimal focus during long imaging sessions remains challenging due to sample drift, thermal fluctuations, and mechanical instabilities.

In this ongoing project, we are developing a deep learning-based approach for real-time focus detection and correction in light sheet microscopy. Our system uses a convolutional neural network to analyze image quality metrics and predict the optimal focus position, which is then used to adjust the microscope stage accordingly.

Status: Ongoing research

Keywords: Light Sheet Microscopy, Deep Learning, Autofocus, Image Quality Assessment

Figure 1: Light Sheet Microscopy Setup

Schematic diagram of our light sheet fluorescence microscopy system with the focus correction module.

Figure 2: Focus Quality Assessment

Examples of in-focus (left) and out-of-focus (right) images of fluorescent beads used for training our focus detection algorithm.

Figure 3: Focus Correction Results

Comparison of image quality metrics before and after applying our focus correction algorithm during a 2-hour time-lapse imaging experiment.

Optical Coherence Tomography Analysis

Tathagata Das, Arijit Ghosh, Mayanglambam Suheshkumar Singh

BnBEng.LAB, School of Physics, Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a non-invasive imaging technique that provides high-resolution cross-sectional images of biological tissues. In this collaborative project, we focused on developing advanced image processing algorithms for OCT image enhancement and feature extraction.

Our work involved implementing noise reduction techniques specifically designed for speckle noise in OCT images, followed by automated segmentation of tissue layers using a combination of traditional image processing methods and machine learning approaches.

Status: Presented at SPIE Photonics West 2024

Keywords: Optical Coherence Tomography, Image Processing, Segmentation, Speckle Noise Reduction
